Frequently Asked Questions

CodeDuels is an online platform where users can engage in coding challenges and compete against others.

We have two play modes: single-player mode and multiplayer mode. In single-player mode, you can play alone and test your coding skills. You cannot earn points in this mode; it's only meant for practice. You can test single-player mode by clicking the button on the home page. In multiplayer mode, you can play against friends. You can send an invitation to your friends or other users on the platform. If you are friends, you can send a duel request, and if it is accepted, the game will launch automatically.

The first player to run a correct code wins. If the code isn't correct, the opponent wins. If the timer runs out and no one has run a correct code, both players lose.

You can delete your account in the settings section by clicking the "Delete my Account" button.

The list of all players on the platform with their number of victories can be found under the "Top Duelists" section on the home page.

Yes, you can track your progress in the 'Profile' section where you can see your completed challenges and ranking.

You can participate in hackathons by navigating to the 'Hackathons' section and registering for any upcoming events.

For any specific questions, feel free to contact us.
